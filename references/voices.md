# Voice Roster — `minimax-speech-2.6-turbo` (pick a `voice_id` per film)

Set `voice.voice_id` in beats.json. Pick the voice that matches the language and tone of your topic.

## Recommended Voices (English)
* **`English_expressive_narrator`** (M, professional, great for documentary/explainers)
* **`English_magnetic_voiced_man`** (M, deep, cinematic)
* **`English_compelling_lady1`** (F, clear, professional)
* **`English_Upbeat_Woman`** (F, energetic, great for advertisements/promos)
* **`English_Trustworth_Man`** (M, corporate, warm, steady)
* **`English_FriendlyPerson`** (Any/Neutral, warm, approachable)
* **`English_CalmWoman`** (F, soft, soothing)
* **`English_ManWithDeepVoice`** (M, very deep, authoritative)
* **`English_Aussie_Bloke`** (M, Australian accent, friendly)
* **`English_PlayfulGirl`** (F, enthusiastic, high energy)

## General Voices
* **`Friendly_Person`** (Default, very versatile)
* **`Deep_Voice_Man`** (Deep, dramatic)
* **`Wise_Woman`** (Mature, authoritative)
* **`Inspirational_girl`** (Bright, motivational)
* **`Calm_Woman`** (Calm, relaxed)
* **`Casual_Guy`** (Informal, natural)
* **`Lively_Girl`** (Bright, bubbly)
* **`Patient_Man`** (Steady, clear)

## Guidelines
1. Keep **one voice** active for the entire video to ensure narrative consistency.
2. Select a voice that matches the tone of your topic (e.g., use `English_expressive_narrator` for serious explainers, and `English_Upbeat_Woman` for product ads).
